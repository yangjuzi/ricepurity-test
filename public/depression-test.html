About the Depression Test

The Depression Test is a mental health screening tool designed to help you assess potential signs of depression. This test is based on common symptoms and indicators used by mental health professionals for initial screening.

How it works: You'll answer 15 questions about your mood, energy levels, sleep patterns, and general outlook on life over recent weeks. Each question explores different aspects of mental health and emotional well-being.

Scoring: Your final result will categorize your depression indicators as either "Minimal," "Mild," "Moderate," "Moderately Severe," or "Severe" based on your responses. The test analyzes your answers across multiple dimensions including mood, energy, motivation, and hope.

Important: This test is for educational purposes only and cannot replace professional medical advice. If you're experiencing persistent sadness, hopelessness, or thoughts of self-harm, please seek help from a mental health professional immediately.

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength. Mental health is just as important as physical health, and there are many effective treatments available. You deserve support and care.
