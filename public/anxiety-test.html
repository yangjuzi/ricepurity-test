About the Anxiety Test

The Anxiety Test is a mental health screening tool designed to help you assess potential signs of anxiety disorders. This test is based on common symptoms and indicators used by mental health professionals for initial screening.

How it works: You'll answer 16 questions about your worry patterns, physical symptoms, and general anxiety levels over recent weeks. Each question explores different aspects of anxiety including generalized worry, social anxiety, and physical manifestations.

Scoring: Your final result will categorize your anxiety indicators as either "Minimal," "Mild," "Moderate," "Moderately Severe," or "Severe" based on your responses. The test analyzes your answers across multiple dimensions including worry level, physical symptoms, calmness, and confidence.

Important: This test is for educational purposes only and cannot replace professional medical advice. If you're experiencing persistent anxiety, panic attacks, or thoughts of self-harm, please seek help from a mental health professional immediately.

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength. Mental health is just as important as physical health, and there are many effective treatments available for anxiety. You deserve support and care.
